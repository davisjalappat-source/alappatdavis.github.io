About Me

I am currently a Academic Associate at the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore, with research experience at the University of Hyderabad, NIUA, and CDS-JNU. My interests include International Trade, Labour Economics, and Development Studies, with a focus on how trade, structural reforms, and technological change shape labour markets and development outcomes.

Recently, I have incorporated machine learning into my research, working with Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). This fusion of deep learning and mathematical modeling is improving the predictive power of solutions to nonlinear differential equations. Alongside this, I have explored fuzzy differential equations and developed mathematical models for SIRS epidemiological systems. These interdisciplinary efforts have helped bridge theory and application in areas like health.

My academic journey began with a Bachelor’s degree in Economics from the University of Calicut, followed by a Master’s in Economics from the University of Hyderabad, where I graduated with first class. During my postgraduate studies, I was mentored by Dr. G. Vijay (Labour Economics) and Dr. G. Sridevi (Public Policy), which shaped my research interests in labour markets, development policy, and urban transformation. I am currently working as an Academic Associate at the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore, where I have served as a Teaching Assistant for Microeconomics and Macroeconomics courses and audited PhD-level Econometrics-I. Previously, I worked as a Research Associate at the National Institute of Urban Affairs (NIUA) on the project “Urban Jobs in Transition – The Phenomenon of Census Towns in Kochi,” where I analysed emerging labour market trends in urbanising regions. My field-based research experience includes work on tribal livelihood security in Kumily, Idukki, with a focus on the gendered impacts of development interventions among the Mannan and Paliyan communities within the Periyar Tiger Reserve. I also interned at the Centre for Development Studies (CDS-JNU), collaborating with ANERT, KSEB, and government agencies for data-driven research on energy and infrastructure policy. These experiences have shaped my research focus on the intersections of labour economics, public policy, and inclusive development, with a growing interest in the socio-economic impacts of technological change, urban transitions, and climate adaptation.
