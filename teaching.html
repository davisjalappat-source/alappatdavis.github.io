Research Focus

My current research focuses on the integration of nonparametric statistical approaches with Physics-Informed Neural Networks. Developing Hybrid-AI quantile regression techniques specifically tailored for spatio-temporal climate data, working to enhance predictive accuracy by blending mathematical rigor with machine learning interpretability. Through this work, building explainable models that capture environmental dynamics through a data-physics synergy. This research continues to advance climate modeling, enabling more robust and accurate forecasts of climate change impacts across various temporal and spatial scales.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe in bridging the gap between theoretical rigor and practical relevance. My classes are designed to stimulate analytical thinking and model-based understanding while demonstrating how abstract mathematics powers real-world problem solving. Aim to empower students with computational tools and critical reasoning skills essential for success in data-driven industries. By connecting mathematical principles to tangible applications, help students develop a deeper appreciation for the discipline and its relevance to contemporary challenges.

Academia

Doctoral Research

Ph.D. in Mathematics, Indian Institute of Information Technology Kottayam (Sep 2020 – Oct 2024)

Under the guidance of Dr. T. Bakkyaraj, my doctoral work centered on my thesis titled Invariant Analysis and Exact Solutions of Certain Nonlinear Fractional Partial Differential and Differential-Difference Equations. My research investigated key areas including fractional differential equations, Lie symmetry analysis, invariant subspace methods, and differential and differential-difference equations. This research not only contributed to the theoretical framework of fractional calculus but also laid a strong foundation for my transition into data-driven methodologies like Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs). Throughout my doctoral studies, I developed novel analytical approaches to solving complex mathematical models with applications in various physical systems.

Postgraduate Research

M.Phil. in Mathematics, Bharathiar University, Coimbatore (Nov 2017 – Dec 2018)

Supervised by Dr. S. Narayanamoorthy, my M.Phil. thesis investigated Glucose Insulin Regulatory Model using Runge-Kutta and Homotopy Perturbation Methods in the Fuzzy Domain. My core coursework included fuzzy mathematics, partial differential equations, and algebra and analysis. This research involved numerical techniques applied within fuzzy systems, enriching my analytical approach toward modeling uncertainty in real-world phenomena. The work combined mathematical modeling with biomedical applications, providing me with a strong interdisciplinary foundation that continues to inform my current research methodologies.
